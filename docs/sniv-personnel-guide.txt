SNIV and Personnel Availability Guide
======================================

What is a SNIV?
---------------
A SNIV is a request submitted by a person indicating they are NOT available to be scheduled.
SNIVs are entered into the scheduling system and flow into the daily schedule automatically.

Personnel Types
---------------
ACTIVE: Full-time staff, available every duty day unless SNIVED or med-down.
AF EXCHANGE: Air Force exchange officer, high scheduling priority.
RESERVIST: Part-time instructor, only available on declared drill days.
NON-PERM: Non-permanent attached pilot, available per individual agreement.

Double Schedule Conflict
------------------------
A double schedule conflict occurs when a SNIVED person still appears on a flight line.
This must be resolved before the duty day begins.
The system flags these conflicts in the double schedule row of the whiteboard.

Med Down
--------
A med-down status means a person is medically grounded and cannot fly.
Med-down personnel are excluded from flight lines and the IP Hotboard.

Reservist Availability
----------------------
Reservists must declare their drill days in advance.
If a reservist is not on a declared drill day they are unavailable.
The scheduling officer maintains the personnel tracker for reservist availability.
