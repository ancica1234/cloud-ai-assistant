Flight Scheduling Guide
=======================

Daily Schedule Build Process
-----------------------------
1. Check environmental data: sunset, EENT, HLL, highlight window, field hours.
2. Review aircraft status: identify FMC, PMC, NMC aircraft and available periods.
3. Pull SNIV list: identify who is unavailable today.
4. Check med-down list: identify medically grounded personnel.
5. Build student priority list: AF exchange first, refreshers second, standard third.
6. Consult IP Hotboard: assign instructor with least flight hours to next line.
7. Assign crews to flight lines: match IP qualifications to event type.
8. Check for double-schedule conflicts.
9. Confirm ground school CSI coverage.
10. Publish the daily whiteboard.

Whiteboard Rows
---------------
ODO: Officer of the Day assigned to each time block.
FCF: Crew assigned to Functional Check Flight.
FLIGHT EVENT: Personnel scheduled for a flight today.
SIM EVENT: Personnel scheduled for a simulator session today.
SNIVED: Personnel who have submitted unavailability.
DOUBLE SCHEDULE: Personnel flagged as conflicting schedules.
MED DOWN: Medically grounded personnel.
GRND EVENT: Personnel assigned to ground events only.

Environmental Data
------------------
Sunset time determines when night operations begin.
EENT defines the night vision goggle requirement threshold.
HLL defines ambient light conditions affecting NVG operations.
Field hours define when the airfield is open for operations.
Flight window (e.g. 2x2) shows available morning and afternoon flight slots.

Aircraft Status Codes
---------------------
FMC: Available for all periods and event types.
PMC: Limited availability, check specific periods.
NMC: Grounded, not available for any flights.
