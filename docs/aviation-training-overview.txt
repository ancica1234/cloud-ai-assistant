Aviation Training Assistant - Knowledge Base Overview
=====================================================

This AI assistant answers questions about aviation flight training operations.
It covers flight scheduling, ground school, instructor availability,
student progress, environmental constraints, and aircraft status.

Key Concepts
------------
SNIV: A request for a person NOT to be scheduled. Sourced from the scheduling system.
FMC: Fully Mission Capable - aircraft available for all flight operations.
PMC: Partially Mission Capable - aircraft has limited availability.
NMC: Non-Mission Capable - aircraft is grounded.
CAL: Calibration flight event type.
FAM: Familiarisation flight event type.
FCF: Functional Check Flight - post-maintenance airworthiness check.
CSI: Certified Simulator Instructor - required for simulator events.
EENT: End of Evening Nautical Twilight - defines night operations window.
HLL: High Light Level - environmental threshold for night operations.
ODO: Officer of the Day - duty officer assigned per time block.
IP: Instructor Pilot - qualified instructor assigned to flight lines.
AF Exchange: Air Force exchange student - high scheduling priority.
Refresher: Pilot returning to currency - elevated scheduling priority.

Scheduling Priority Order
--------------------------
1. AF Exchange students - tightest timelines, scheduled first.
2. Refresher pilots - returning to currency, scheduled second.
3. Standard syllabus students - scheduled after higher priorities.

Flight Lines
------------
Flight lines are mission slots assigned per period (AM, PM, EVE).
Each line has: MSN number, brief time, event type, aircraft, IP, and student.
Hot seat: same aircraft transitions from AM to PM line without shutdown.

Ground School
-------------
Ground school events run alongside flight operations.
Some events require a CSI to be present (simulator labs).
Some events involve outside agencies (aeromed, night lab, course rules briefs).

Instructor Hotboard
-------------------
The IP Hotboard ranks instructors by least year-to-date flight hours.
Instructor with fewest hours gets priority for the next flight assignment.
Reservists are only available on their declared drill days.
Instructors who are SNIVED or med-down are excluded from the hotboard.
